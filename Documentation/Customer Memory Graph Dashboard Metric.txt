CUSTOMER MEMORY GRAPH 


A. CUSTOMER BEHAVIOR PATTERNS

1. Repeat Issue Rate % of customers who have raised the same intent more than once in the configured time window. Distinguishes between customers with genuinely recurring problems vs one-time contacts. A high repeat issue rate at the base level signals a systemic product or service failure, not a support failure. Aggregate form: % of customer base · ranked by most repeated intent · period-over-period trend. Also classify the intent.

2. Issue Gravity Score Classifies the severity of the issue raised, not just its category. A refund request for ₹200 and a refund request for ₹20,000 are both "refund" intents, but they carry different customer impact. Combines issue type, order value, number of prior attempts to resolve, and current sentiment into a gravity score. Aggregate form: Distribution of tickets by gravity band (Low / Medium / High / Critical) · by intent and channel Add configuration 

3. Issue Gravity Escalation Pattern Tracks whether a customer's issues are getting more severe over time — from informational queries to complaints to escalations. A customer who started with "order status" and is now raising "legal threat" intents is on a measurable deterioration trajectory. Distinct from Issue Gravity Score, which is a point-in-time measure — this tracks the directional change over time. Aggregate form: % of customers showing issue gravity escalation · avg number of tickets before gravity shift · by segment and product category Add configuration 

4. Customer Contact Velocity Rate at which individual customers are increasing their contact frequency. A customer who contacted once a month and is now contacting weekly is a leading churn signal — often before sentiment visibly turns negative. Measures acceleration, not just volume. Aggregate form: % of base with accelerating contact frequency · distribution of velocity bands · week-over-week (High/Medium/Low Contact velocity and the configuration)

5. Self-Resolution Dropout Rate % of customers who attempted self-service (IVR, chatbot, knowledge base) before reaching a live agent and failed (handover to agent). Inferred from channel switching behavior and opening intent ("I already tried X"). Indicates where the self-service experience is breaking down for real customers. Aggregate form: % of tickets preceded by a self-service attempt · by self-service channel · by intent

6. Channel Switching Behavior Tracks how many customers switch support channels within the same issue journey — e.g. started on chat, moved to voice, then email. Each switch represents a failure to resolve on the previous channel and compounds customer effort. Aggregate form: % of customers who switched channels for the same issue · most common switch paths · avg channels used per issue

7. Silent Dissatisfaction Rate Customers whose conversation sentiment and post-resolution sentiment are both negative but who submitted no CSAT, raised no escalation, and did not reopen the ticket. These customers leave without signaling — the highest churn risk cohort. Aggregate form: % of base fitting this profile · trend over time

8. Customer Loyalty Erosion Score Composite score per customer that tracks deterioration in their relationship with the brand across tickets — combining worsening sentiment trend, increasing contact frequency, decreasing CSAT, and shift toward higher-gravity intents. Aggregate form: Distribution of customers by erosion band · count moving into high-erosion band each period

9. Issue Resolution Satisfaction Gap Difference between the resolution the customer explicitly asked for and the resolution they actually received — inferred from transcript. For example, customer asked for a full refund and received a coupon. Measures how often customers get what they came for. Aggregate form: % of tickets with a resolution gap · by intent and resolution type · correlated with post-resolution sentiment

10. Post-Resolution Sentiment Drop Rate % of customers whose sentiment gets worse after the resolution is provided — indicating the resolution itself disappointed them, even if the ticket was technically closed. A distinct and more precise signal than overall negative sentiment. Aggregate form: % of tickets showing sentiment drop at resolution point · by resolution type and intent

11. Issue Persistence Score Measures how consistently a customer contacts for the same intent over time. A high score means the customer keeps returning with the same unresolved category of issue. Calculated as the ratio of the dominant intent's ticket count to total ticket count for that customer, aggregated across the base. Aggregate form: Distribution of customers by persistence score · high score = unresolved systemic issue · by intent category

12. Resolution Acceptance Rate % of tickets where the customer explicitly or implicitly accepted the resolution — inferred from conversation (no rebuttal after resolution offer, positive closing sentiment, no reopen within 7 days). Aggregate form: % accepted vs closed-but-rejected · by resolution type and intent

B. CUSTOMER SENTIMENT INTELLIGENCE

1. Customer Sentiment Distribution Snapshot of how the entire customer base feels across all tickets in the configured window — segmented into Positive, Neutral, and Negative cohorts. The foundational population-level sentiment metric. Aggregate form: Count and % in each band · filterable by segment, channel, product, and time period · trended

2. Sentiment Transition Matrix Tracks how customer sentiment shifts between their consecutive tickets — not within a single ticket but across their journey. Captures how many customers moved Positive → Negative, Negative → Positive, Neutral → Negative, etc. Aggregate form: 3×3 matrix (Positive / Neutral / Negative) showing customer counts in each transition cell · period-over-period comparison

3. Sentiment Drift Throughout the Journey Measures net sentiment change from the first turn to the last turn across all tickets. Separately tracks sentiment at four stages: ticket open, mid-conversation, post-resolution offer, and ticket close. Shows exactly where in the conversation customers emotionally shift. Aggregate form: Avg sentiment score at each journey stage across all tickets · by intent and channel

4. Emotional Intensity Distribution Beyond positive/neutral/negative — classifies the strength of customer emotion in the transcript. Distinguishes between mildly negative and severely distressed, or mildly positive and genuinely delighted. LLM labels each ticket across five intensity tiers: Calm / Mildly Frustrated / Frustrated / Distressed / Irate. Aggregate form: % of customers in each intensity band · % shifting to Distressed or Irate mid-conversation · by intent

5. Frustration Trigger Mapping Identifies the specific moments or statements in conversations that caused a measurable negative sentiment shift — e.g. "your policy states" or "we can't process that." Clusters these triggers across all tickets to surface the most common frustration-inducing interactions. Aggregate form: Ranked list of frustration triggers by frequency · avg sentiment drop per trigger · by intent and channel

6. Customer Tone Shift Rate % of tickets where the customer's communication tone changed significantly during the conversation — from polite to aggressive, or from aggressive to calm. Tone shifts are distinct from sentiment shifts and capture a customer's direct behavioral response to what they heard mid-ticket. Aggregate form: % of tickets with detected tone shift · direction of shift (positive or negative) · by intent 

C. CUSTOMER LOYALTY & CHURN INTELLIGENCE

1. Churn Risk Distribution Count and % of customers in High / Medium / Low churn risk bands. The top-level population health metric for retention teams. Aggregate form: Absolute counts per band · movement between bands period-over-period · by customer segment and tenure

2. Pre-Churn Behavioral Signature Pattern of behaviors observed in customers who actually churned in the 60–90 days before they left. Used to build a recognizable fingerprint — e.g. 3+ reopens, negative post-resolution sentiment, contact velocity increase, low CSAT — that can be applied to identify at-risk customers in the current active base. Aggregate form: Signature profile with key signals and their frequency in pre-churn cohort · % of current active customers matching the signature

3. High-Value Customer Risk Concentration Churn risk distribution segmented by customer value tier — inferred from conversation data such as order value, tenure, and product tier. Identifies whether high-risk customers are disproportionately concentrated in the high-value segment, which carries greater revenue impact. Aggregate form: Churn risk band × customer value tier cross-tab · revenue at risk estimate where order data is available

4. Recovery Rate % of customers who were previously in the high churn risk band and moved to Medium or Low in the subsequent period — indicating improved experience or successful intervention. Measures whether conditions are actually getting better for at-risk customers. Aggregate form: % of previously high-risk customers who de-escalated · period-over-period

D. INTENT & JOURNEY INTELLIGENCE

1. Intent Shift Analysis Tracks how a customer's intent changes turn-by-turn within a single conversation — mapping the journey from opening intent to closing intent. Identifies how often customers arrive with one problem and surface additional ones mid-conversation, and which intents most commonly trigger secondary intents. Aggregate form: Most common intent shift paths across all tickets · % of tickets with intent shift · avg number of intents per ticket

2. Unresolved Intent Accumulation Tracks customers who have raised the same intent across multiple tickets without a confirmed resolution. Distinguished from Repeat Issue Rate by focusing specifically on the absence of resolution — the issue was raised, tickets were closed, but the underlying problem persists. Aggregate form: Count of customers with 2+ unresolved instances of the same intent · by intent category · avg number of attempts before resolution

3. Intent Complexity Score Measures how complex a customer's support needs are, based on the number of distinct intents they've raised, whether those intents required escalation or multi-agent handling. Intents that don't fit standard categories are mapped under Miscellaneous. Aggregate form: Distribution of customers by intent complexity band · by customer tenure and segment

4. Repeat Contact Interval Trend Tracks whether the time gap between a customer's consecutive tickets is shrinking, stable, or growing over time. Shrinking intervals — even at low ticket volume — signal mounting dissatisfaction and increasing dependency on support, both predictors of churn. Aggregate form: % of base with shrinking interval trend · avg interval by risk band · period-over-period

5. Customer Journey Abandonment Rate % of customers who disengaged mid-conversation — dropped the call, closed the chat, or stopped responding — before a resolution was reached. Abandonment is a distinct dissatisfaction signal, separate from negative CSAT or reopens, and is often the strongest indicator of immediate churn risk. Aggregate form: % of tickets ending in abandonment · by channel, intent, and time-in-conversation at abandonment · correlated with churn risk

6. Resolution Journey Stage Breakdown Total handle time decomposed into distinct phases: Greeting → Probing → Hold → Resolution Execution → Closing. Each phase is timed individually. Allows identification of which phase is the primary contributor to negative customer sentiment rather than treating handle time as a single undifferentiated block. Aggregate form: Avg time per phase across all tickets · by intent · stacked time breakdown.
